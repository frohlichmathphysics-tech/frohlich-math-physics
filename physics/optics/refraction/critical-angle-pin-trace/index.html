How to use this lab

  1. Aim the laser and sweep. Drag the laser pen along its arc (0°–80° from the normal). Watch the refracted beam below the flat face. As you increase the angle, the refracted beam gets dimmer and spreads wider until, at some angle, it disappears entirely — that’s the critical angle. Switch colours with the round buttons; each colour has its own critical angle.
  2. Find the threshold and place pins. Set the laser to the angle at which the refracted beam just vanishes for the active colour. Click on the canvas to drop two pins along the in-water incident beam (between the curved face and the vertex). Right-click a pin or drag it off the canvas to remove it.
  3. Lift the dish. Press Lift dish to fade the apparatus and reveal the paper trace (flat face, curved-face arc, vertex, normal). Pins stay where you placed them.
  4. Trace the rays. Turn on Ray 1 and align it through your two incident-beam pins. Turn on Ray 2 and lay it along the flat face — at the critical angle, the refracted ray skims the surface (θ₂ = 90°).
  5. Measure θc. Turn on the Protractor, place its centre on the vertex, and align its baseline with the flat face. Read θc as the angle between Ray 1 (incident) and the normal — equivalently, 90° minus the angle between Ray 1 and Ray 2.
  6. Record the trial. Enter θc and your reading uncertainty Δθc, then press Add row. Repeat for each colour (and as many times per colour as you wish — multiple trials give you spread for Δθc if you want it).
  7. Reveal results. After at least one trial of any colour, click Reveal results, enter your value(s), and compare with the applet’s expected critical angle for each colour.
