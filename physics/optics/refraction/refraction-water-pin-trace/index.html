How to use this lab

  1. Aim the laser. Drag the laser pen along its arc to choose an incidence angle (0°–80° from the normal). Suggested angles: 0°, 20°, 35°, 50°, 60°, 70°. Use the colour buttons to switch wavelength. The laser stays on each colour's beam until you toggle Laser [OFF].
  2. Place pins. Click on the canvas to drop pins along the incident beam (above the dish) and the exit beam (below the dish) — typically 2 pins on each side. Right-click a pin or drag it off the canvas to remove it.
  3. Lift the dish. Press Lift dish to fade the apparatus and reveal the paper trace (flat face, vertex, normal). Pins stay where you placed them.
  4. Trace the rays. Turn on Ray 1 and Ray 2. Each ray-line appears with two square endpoint handles. Drag either handle to align the line with its pin pair — the line passes through both handles and extends to the canvas edges.
  5. Measure angles. Turn on the Protractor, drag it so its centre sits at the vertex, and rotate as needed. Read θ₁ between Ray 1 and the normal, and θ₂ between Ray 2 and the normal.
  6. Record the trial. Enter θ₁, Δθ₁, θ₂, Δθ₂ in the Trial data form and press Add row. Repeat at different angles (and colours, if doing dispersion).
  7. Reveal results. After 3+ trials of any colour, calculate n using your teacher's method, then click Reveal results and enter your value to compare with the applet's check and the expected index.
